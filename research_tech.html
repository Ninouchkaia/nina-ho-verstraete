Vision & Overview

I am a bioinformatics researcher and data scientist specializing in functional genomics, computational modeling, and biological data integration. My work bridges academic research and industrial applications, with a focus on life sciences, complex systems, and technological innovation.

Research Themes

Cancer Research & Immunotherapy

  • Experience – INSERM (2019–2023): Agent-based modeling of tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs) in chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), tumor microenvironment deconvolution, multi-omics integration in AML (LungPredict project).
  • Publication: Verstraete et al. (2023). Agent-based model of monocyte differentiation in CLL. iScience.
  • Publication: Marku et al. (2020). Boolean model of macrophage polarization. Cancers.
  • Publication: Xie et al. (2021). GEM-DeCan for immune profiling. bioRxiv.

Network Medicine & COVID-19

  • Experience – INSERM (2019–2023): Integration of proteins, diseases, drugs, and symptoms for COVID-19 via CovMulNet19; modeling SARS-CoV-2 interactome using multiscale physics.
  • Publication: Verstraete et al. (2020). CovMulNet19: COVID-19 network medicine. Netw Syst Med.
  • Publication: Ghavasieh et al. (2021). Pan-viral interactome & SARS-CoV-2. Commun Phys.

Systems Biology & Modeling

  • Experience – INSERM: Agent-based simulations using NetLogo & OpenMole, model-based interpretation of experimental results in oncology.
  • Publication: Verstraete et al. (2022). Model of TAM differentiation in CLL. ESCS Symposium.
  • Publication: Verstraete et al. (2020). CovMulNet19 integrates network layers. Netw Syst Med.

Transcriptional Regulation & Structural Bioinformatics

  • Publication: Verstraete et al. (2012). PhD Thesis. Paris 6.
  • Publication: Kuzmina et al. (2014). Cyclin T1 interaction dynamics. Retrovirology.
  • Publication: Marz et al. (2009). Evolution of 7SK RNA. Mol Biol Evol.

Protein Families & Structural Bioinformatics
